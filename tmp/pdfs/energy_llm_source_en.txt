and Future Perspectives
Chaobo Zhanga, Jian Zhangb, Jie Lub, Yang Zhaob,c,*

H I G H L I G H T S
•Applications of large language models (LLMs) in energy systems are reviewed.
•Advantages and disadvantages of LLMs used in energy systems are discussed.
•Five performance enhancement technologies for LLMs are summarized.
•Thirteen roles of LLMs in energy systems are uncovered.
•Opportunities, challenges, and eight potential future directions are presented.
A R T I C L E I N F O A B S T R A C T

Knowledge augmentation aims to enhance the performance of LLMs
by integrating external knowledge into the prompting process. To achieve this, it is essential to develop and continuously update an external
knowledge base. The energy domain already has many available
knowledge sources, such as design documents, energy policies, textbooks, research papers, and equipment maintenance records. They can
be utilized to develop external knowledge bases.
RAG is the most popular knowledge augmentation technique in energy system applications. As shown in Fig. 9, RAG retrieves relevant
information based on user queries from external knowledge bases (such
as text documents, web content, and knowledge graphs) and incorporates this information into the prompts [69]. The prompts, which
include both the user queries and the retrieved information, are then
input into LLMs to generate more accurate responses. RAG has been
widely used to enhance the performance of LLMs in energy systems. For
instance, Labib designed a RAG-based framework to enable LLMs to
answer questions related to the use of two widely adopted building
energy simulation tools: EnergyPlus and OpenStudio [70]. A total of 59
